Philippines
The Philippines has indeed become increasingly vulnerable to
drought, desertification and land degradation brought about by many
contributing factors, foremost is the increasing recurrence of dry
spell and alternating incidence of El Nino and La Nina
as well as poor management of land, freshwater and water
resources.
Inner Mongolia
China
Inner Mongolia is one of the regions in China with the most concentration of desertified and sandy land. The area of desertified land has reached nearly 61 million hectares, accounting for 23.3 percent of the country's total desertified land area. Sandy land covers an area of 40.8 million hectares, making up
23.7 percent of the country's total. In the past five years,
Inner Mongolia has treated nearly 4.8 million hectares of desertified
and sandy land, accounting for over 40 percent of the country's
total treated area during the period.
Murray-Darling Basin
Australia
The Murray-Darling Basin, encompassing the Murray, Darling, and Murrumbidgee rivers across several Australian states, is vital for rural communities and the economy, with approximately three million people dependent on its freshwater, primarily utilized by the agricultural sector for irrigation.
Human activity and persistent drought conditions pose significant
threats, leading to the basin's diminishing size. Spatial analysis
reveals the substantial impact of human land use on the basin's
ecosystem, presenting ongoing and future challenges.
Sahel Region
Australia
In the second half of the 20th century, the Sahel region experienced a rapid increase in population, leading to intensified human activities and greater demands on the land than ever before. This population growth, combined with unsustainable land use practices such as overgrazing, deforestation, and expansion of
agricultural land, exacerbated soil erosion and desertification
across the Sahel.
